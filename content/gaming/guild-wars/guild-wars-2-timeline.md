**Guild Wars 2**, released in 2012, is set roughly two and a half centuries after the original game, in a Tyria where the **Elder Dragons** the first game only hinted at have woken in force. Its story is told through a persistent base game, recurring episodic releases called the **Living World**, and a series of expansions; together they carry a single continuous arc from the fall of the dragon Zhaitan to the close of the Elder Dragon era and beyond. This entry traces that arc chronologically, beginning with the gap between the two games and continuing through the sixth expansion.

In-world dates use the Mouvelian calendar's After the Exodus (AE) reckoning, as in the original game. The annual expansions released from 2023 onward are placed only approximately on the in-world timeline; ArenaNet loosened the tight calendar-to-release correspondence it had maintained through *Heart of Thorns*.

## The Lost Centuries (1080–1325 AE)

Roughly two and a half centuries separate the two games, a span covered in tie-in novels and the sequel's opening rather than by any single release.

### The dragons wake

Across this period the Elder Dragons woke in earnest. The fire dragon **Primordus** and the undersea dragon **Zhaitan** stirred; Zhaitan raised the long-drowned kingdom of Orr as an undead horde, fouling the seas and driving whole peoples from their homes.[^gw2timeline] The waking dragons, and the magic they consumed, reshaped the balance of the world that the original game had left intact.

### A changed Tyria

By 1325 AE the map had been redrawn. The charr now hold their ancestral Ascalon, the human kingdom has retreated to Kryta, and five peoples share an uneasy world: humans, charr, norn, asura, and the plant-born **sylvari**, a race that did not exist during the first game.[^gw2main] These five are the playable races of the sequel.

## Guild Wars 2: the Pact and the fall of Zhaitan (1325 AE)

The base game opens in 1325 AE and brings the Elder Dragon threat, long deferred, to a head.[^gw2main]

### The personal story

Each character begins with a personal story rooted in their race and background before converging on the central crisis: **Zhaitan**, the undead dragon whose risen armies pour out of Orr. The early game establishes the three great orders, the Durmand Priory, the Vigil, and the Order of Whispers, that the player must unite.

### Forming the Pact and the assault on Orr

Under the sylvari marshal Trahearne, the orders combine into the **Pact**, a unified army aimed at the dragon. The campaign culminates in an assault on Orr that brings down Zhaitan, the first Elder Dragon slain in the modern age and proof that the dragons can be killed.[^gw2main]

## Living World Season 1: Scarlet's war and the fall of Lion's Arch (1325–1326 AE)

The first season of the **Living World** introduced the game's episodic storytelling and centered on **Scarlet Briar**, a brilliant, unhinged sylvari who united disparate enemy factions into a single threat. Her campaign of escalating sabotage ended with a direct assault on Lion's Arch, the hub city, which was largely destroyed.[^gw2timeline] Scarlet was killed in the attack, but her true purpose, tapping a ley line beneath the city, helped rouse the next dragon.

## Living World Season 2: the sylvari secret and the Mordrem (1326–1327 AE)

The second season formed the survivors into the guild Dragon's Watch and turned toward the jungle dragon **Mordremoth**, whose Mordrem forces began to stir. The season's central revelation, that the sylvari were created by Mordremoth and could be turned to its will, reframed an entire playable race and set up the first expansion. It closed with the firstborn Caithe taking the egg of the dragon Glint into the deep jungle.

## Heart of Thorns: the death of Mordremoth (1328 AE)

The first expansion follows the Pact into the Heart of Maguuma to face Mordremoth directly.[^gw2hot]

### The Pact fleet and the Heart of Maguuma

As the campaign opens, Mordremoth destroys the Pact's airship fleet, stranding and scattering the army deep in a hostile jungle.[^gw2hot] The survivors fight their way through the canopy and the Mordrem to regroup and reach the dragon.

### Gliding, elite specializations, and Mordremoth's defeat

*Heart of Thorns* introduced gliding and the first **elite specializations**, which expanded each profession with a new role. The campaign against Mordremoth, recorded in-world as beginning on 30 Zephyr 1328 AE and lasting roughly six weeks, ends with the dragon's defeat, though at the cost of Trahearne's life.[^gw2hot]

## Living World Season 3: the White Mantle and Aurene (1328–1330 AE)

The third season opened on the loss of a companion and the return of old enemies: the **White Mantle** and the mursaat Lazarus, both rooted in the original game's lore. Its larger significance is the hatching of **Aurene**, the scion of Glint's egg, who bonds with the player's character and becomes central to everything that follows. The season also brought the first hints that a far older enemy, the god Balthazar, was moving in disguise.

## Path of Fire: the death of a god (1330 AE)

The second expansion turns from dragons to the gods, and sends the player to the long-isolated lands of the south.[^gw2main]

### Balthazar, Kralkatorrik, and the Crystal Desert

The human god of war, **Balthazar**, escaped from a divine prison, hunts the crystal dragon **Kralkatorrik** to consume its power and remake the world. Pursuing him, the player travels through the Crystal Desert and into Elona, the realm last seen in *Nightfall*.

### Mounts and Balthazar's defeat

*Path of Fire* introduced raised **mounts** as a core traversal system. Balthazar is killed in the campaign, but his death releases a surge of magic into Kralkatorrik and Aurene, leaving the dragon threat worse than before.

## Living World Season 4: the fall of Joko and Kralkatorrik (1330–1332 AE)

The fourth season resolved two long-running threats. The undead lich-king **Palawa Joko**, freed at the end of the original game's *Nightfall*, returns to power in Elona and is finally killed. The season then closes with the death of Kralkatorrik at Dragonfall, after which Aurene becomes the first dragon able to absorb a slain Elder Dragon's magic without being corrupted by it, a turning point for the whole conflict.

## The Icebrood Saga: Jormag and Primordus (1333–1335 AE)

The fifth season, presented as a standalone saga, built toward a collision between the ice dragon **Jormag** and the fire dragon Primordus, opposing forces that could be turned against each other. Set against a charr civil conflict, the saga ends with both Elder Dragons brought down together, leaving only a handful of the original dragons unaccounted for.

## End of Dragons: the close of the Dragon Cycle (1335 AE)

The third expansion reopens a continent shut off since before the sequel and brings the dragon storyline to its conclusion.[^gw2main]

### The return to Cantha

The empire of **Cantha**, isolated for roughly two and a half centuries, opens its borders again, and the player travels there amid political intrigue and rapid technological change. The expansion introduced fishing, skiffs, the Siege Turtle mount, and a third elite specialization for each profession.[^gw2main]

### Soo-Won, the Void, and Aurene's new role

In Cantha the player confronts the deepest truth of the setting: the Elder Dragons exist to bind a corrupting force called the **Void**, and the eldest of them, Soo-Won, once divided the world's magic among the others. To stop the Void from overrunning Tyria, Soo-Won is euthanized and Aurene takes on the remaining dragon magics, ending the Elder Dragon era and the franchise's long **Dragon Cycle**.[^gw2timeline]

## Secrets of the Obscure: beyond the dragons (~1336 AE)

The fourth expansion is the first major story set after the dragons, and it opens the setting onto new cosmology.[^gw2timeline]

### The Kryptis and the Wizard's Tower

A long-hidden order of mages, the **Astral Ward**, and their Wizard's Tower come to the fore as a new enemy arrives: the demonic **Kryptis**, led by the Eparch, who breach from their realm into Tyria.[^gw2timeline] The expansion ties this threat back to the elder races and wizards introduced in the game's deep backstory.

### A new model

*Secrets of the Obscure* began a cadence of smaller annual expansions supported by quarterly updates, replacing the older pattern of large expansions and free Living World seasons. It also introduced account-wide flight and weaponmaster training, which loosened long-standing restrictions on builds and traversal.[^gw2main]

## Janthir Wilds: the isles and homesteading (~1336–1337 AE)

The fifth expansion sends players to the **Isles of Janthir**, drawing in the Lowland Kodan and the titans woven through the setting's older lore. Its signature additions are player **homesteading**, a persistent personal space, and the land spear as a new weapon, alongside the warclaw mount's use in open-world play.

## Visions of Eternity: Castora and the Inquest (~1337 AE)

The sixth and current expansion, released in October 2025, takes the Commander to the hidden island of **Castora** to uncover and stop a scheme by the **Inquest**, the asuran research collective that has served as an antagonist since the base game.[^arenanet2025voe] It continued the post-dragon direction set by the two expansions before it.

## The future

Guild Wars 3 has been referenced as the next mainline game in the franchise, though it remains early and details are limited.[^gw2main]
