This entry continues the **Warcraft** chronology from Year 0 — the opening of the **Dark Portal** — through the present day. Where the era before the portal is reconstructed largely from mythology and backstory, the era after it is dramatized directly: each major period corresponds to a Blizzard game, beginning with the *Warcraft* real-time strategy trilogy and continuing through *World of Warcraft* and its eleven expansions.[^timeline2026] The companion entry, *Warcraft Timeline: Before the Dark Portal*, covers everything prior to this point.[^wowpedia2026]

Dates are given as years After the Dark Portal (ADP) where the sources fix them, and as real-world release years once the live game's internal dating grows loose.

## The First War

In Year 0, the orcish **Horde** crossed the Dark Portal into the swamps of **Azeroth** and fell upon the human kingdom of Stormwind. The conflict, dramatized in *Warcraft: Orcs & Humans*, ended in catastrophe for the humans: the Guardian **Medivh** was slain by his apprentice Khadgar once his corruption was exposed, and the half-orc Garona, magically compelled, assassinated King Llane. **Stormwind** fell to fire, and the surviving refugees fled north under Anduin Lothar.[^timeline2026]

## The Second War

By Year 4, Lothar's refugees had rallied the human kingdoms, and under the new warchief Orgrim Doomhammer the Horde pushed north in the war depicted by *Warcraft II: Tides of Darkness*. The orcs enslaved the red dragonflight using the Demon Soul and overran Khaz Modan, but the conflict turned when Gul'dan abandoned the siege of Lordaeron's Capital City to seek the Tomb of Sargeras for himself.[^timeline2026]

### Formation of the Alliance of Lordaeron

The human-led response coalesced into the **Alliance of Lordaeron**, uniting the seven human kingdoms with the high elves, dwarves, and gnomes. The Order of the Silver Hand was founded and its first paladins trained. With Gul'dan's defection fracturing the Horde, the Alliance prevailed; Doomhammer was captured, and Khadgar destroyed the Dark Portal, though a rift between the worlds remained.[^timeline2026]

## Beyond the Dark Portal and the fall of Draenor

Around Years 7–8, the orcs not killed in the war were confined to internment camps while the elder shaman Ner'zhul reopened the portal from the Draenor side, as told in *Warcraft II: Beyond the Dark Portal*. Seeking new worlds to conquer, Ner'zhul gathered artifacts of immense power and tore open numerous dimensional rifts at once. The resulting cataclysm shattered Draenor into the broken remnant later known as **Outland**.[^timeline2026]

### The birth of the Lich King

Fleeing through the rifts, Ner'zhul was captured by Kil'jaeden, stripped of his body, and twisted into the **Lich King** — a disembodied will bound within a block of ice in distant Northrend and tasked with raising an undead army, the Scourge, to prepare Azeroth for the Legion's return.[^timeline2026]

## The rising darkness

In the interwar decades, the young orc **Thrall** escaped captivity and rebuilt a new, honor-bound Horde free of demonic corruption, while the human Alliance splintered under the cost of maintaining its internment camps. In the shadows, the necromancer Kel'Thuzad heeded the Lich King's call and founded the Cult of the Damned, seeding the plague that would soon consume Lordaeron.[^timeline2026]

## The Third War

Around Year 20, the Lich King's plague of undeath swept across Lordaeron in the events of *Warcraft III: Reign of Chaos*. The **Scourge** rose as a tide of undead, and the Burning Legion soon followed, with the demon Archimonde leading an invasion of Kalimdor.[^timeline2026]

### The fall of Arthas

Prince **Arthas** Menethil, sent to stem the plague, descended into atrocity at Stratholme and pursued the dreadlord Mal'Ganis to Northrend, where he claimed the cursed runeblade **Frostmourne**. The blade consumed his soul; he returned home a death knight, murdered his father King Terenas, and brought Lordaeron to ruin before traveling north to serve the Lich King directly.[^timeline2026]

### The Battle of Mount Hyjal

The orcs, night elves, and humans set aside their enmity to make a final stand at **Mount Hyjal**. Channeling the energies of the World Tree and an army of wisps, the defenders destroyed Archimonde and halted the Legion's invasion — a victory that laid the groundwork for the uneasy coexistence of the modern factions.[^timeline2026]

## Rise of the Lich King

In the years following, dramatized in *Warcraft III: The Frozen Throne*, Illidan Stormrage fled to Outland to escape Kil'jaeden's wrath, and the banshee Sylvanas Windrunner broke free of the Lich King's control to found the **Forsaken**. The arc closed when Arthas ascended the Frozen Throne and merged with Ner'zhul, becoming a single, unrivaled Lich King.[^timeline2026]

## The age of the adventurer: World of Warcraft

Around Year 25, the original *World of Warcraft* (2004) opened the live era of the franchise, shifting the perspective from commanders to individual adventurers. With the great wars settled into a tense Alliance–Horde standoff, the Old Gods and their servants stirred across a recovering Azeroth: the firelord Ragnaros in the Molten Core, the broodmother Onyxia, the black dragon Nefarian, and the awakened Old God C'Thun beneath Ahn'Qiraj. A first Scourge incursion and the necropolis of Naxxramas foreshadowed the war to come.[^timeline2026]

## The Burning Crusade

The first **expansion**, *The Burning Crusade* (2007), reopened the Dark Portal and carried players into the shattered realm of Outland. There the Alliance and Horde confronted the fallen Illidan, the corrupted prince Kael'thas Sunstrider, and ultimately the demon lord Kil'jaeden at the reignited Sunwell. The expansion added the draenei to the Alliance and the blood elves to the Horde.[^timeline2026]

## Wrath of the Lich King

*Wrath of the Lich King* (2008) brought the war to the frozen continent of Northrend and the long-foreshadowed confrontation with Arthas. Along the way, adventurers uncovered the titan complex of Ulduar and contained the Old God Yogg-Saron.[^timeline2026]

### The fall of the Lich King

The campaign culminated in the assault on Icecrown Citadel, where Arthas was finally killed. To prevent the Scourge from running wild without a master, the paladin Bolvar Fordragon took up the Frozen Throne, becoming a new and benevolent Lich King.[^timeline2026]

## Cataclysm

*Cataclysm* (2010) saw the corrupted Dragon Aspect **Deathwing** erupt from the world's core, physically reshaping the face of Azeroth and loosing the Old Gods' elemental servants across the reforged continents. The expansion overhauled the original game world and added the worgen to the Alliance and the goblins to the Horde.[^timeline2026]

## Mists of Pandaria

*Mists of Pandaria* (2012) lifted the ten-thousand-year veil on the hidden continent of Pandaria, introducing its pandaren inhabitants and the emotion-feeding Sha. The expansion's central thread was the descent of warchief Garrosh Hellscream into tyranny, ending in the Siege of Orgrimmar, where a coalition of both factions deposed him.[^timeline2026]

## Warlords of Draenor

*Warlords of Draenor* (2014) is a deliberate departure from the main chronology: Garrosh escapes into the past of an **alternate-timeline Draenor**, preventing the orcs from drinking the Blood of Mannoroth and forging instead the technologically advanced Iron Horde. Because it unfolds on a separate timeline branch, its events sit apart from the main ADP count even as they reintroduce figures such as Grommash Hellscream, Gul'dan, and Archimonde.[^timeline2026]

## Legion

*Legion* (2016) staged the Burning Legion's largest and most desperate invasion, centered on the Broken Isles, the night-elven city of Suramar, and the Tomb of Sargeras, before carrying the fight to the Legion's homeworld of Argus. Its defining event came when Sargeras, defeated, drove a colossal sword into Azeroth itself — a wound that began bleeding the potent substance Azerite and set the stage for the next conflict.[^timeline2026]

## Battle for Azeroth

*Battle for Azeroth* (2018) reignited open war between the factions over the newly precious Azerite, opening the kingdoms of Kul Tiras to the Alliance and Zandalar to the Horde. The expansion ended by freeing the last imprisoned Old God, **N'Zoth**, into the world.[^timeline2026]

## Shadowlands

*Shadowlands* (2020) followed the heroes of Azeroth beyond death into the realms of the afterlife — Bastion, Maldraxxus, Revendreth, Ardenweald, and the prison-realm of the Maw. The expansion revealed the architect behind much of the saga's suffering in **the Jailer**, Zovaal, and tied the franchise's cosmology back to its mythological origins.[^timeline2026]

## Dragonflight

*Dragonflight* (2022) returned to the newly awakened Dragon Isles, restoring the diminished Dragon Aspects and confronting the Primalist followers of the ancient Incarnates — the same conflict, echoed across twenty thousand years, that the *War of the Scaleborn* backstory had set in motion. The expansion marked a deliberate return to grounded, world-focused storytelling.[^timeline2026]

## The Worldsoul Saga

Announced in 2023, the **Worldsoul Saga** is a planned three-expansion arc built around the world-soul of Azeroth and the cosmic forces converging on it.[^roadmap2026]

### The War Within

*The War Within* (2024) opened the saga beneath the surface, in the subterranean continent of Khaz Algar, with regions such as Hallowfall and the Nerub-ar Palace. Its antagonist, Xal'atath, advances a Void agenda against the slumbering world-soul that the saga places at its heart.[^timeline2026]

### Midnight

*Midnight* (2026), the saga's second chapter, was released on March 3, 2026, after an early-access opening on February 27.[^midnight2026] It centers the conflict between the Light and the Void on the elven homeland of Quel'Thalas and the forests of Eversong, and it introduced long-requested **player housing** to the game.[^midnight2026]

### The Last Titan

The saga is set to conclude with *The Last Titan*, announced alongside the other two chapters but not yet dated, which is expected to return to Northrend and resolve the world-soul storyline.[^roadmap2026]
