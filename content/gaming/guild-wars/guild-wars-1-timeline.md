**Guild Wars**, released in 2005, is the original entry in ArenaNet's franchise: three standalone campaigns, *Prophecies*, *Factions*, and *Nightfall*, followed by the expansion *Eye of the North*. Its story moves across the continent of Tyria and the distant lands of Cantha and Elona, and it establishes the mythological and political foundations that *Guild Wars 2* later builds on. This entry traces that story chronologically, from the world's mythic origins through the close of the original game's content around 1080 AE; the sequel's era is covered in a companion entry.

In-world dates use the **Mouvelian calendar**, Tyria's standard reckoning, which counts years from the Exodus of the Gods as year zero, in years Before the Exodus (BE) and After the Exodus (AE).[^gw2mouvelian] Where a date is given as a real-world year in parentheses, it refers to the game's release rather than its in-fiction setting.

## The Mythic Age

Long before the human kingdoms, Tyria's history was shaped by the **Elder Dragons** and a handful of ancient races. Only fragments of this period survive in legend, and exact dates are uncertain.[^gw2timeline]

### The Elder Dragons and the elder races

By its own oldest legends, the world of Tyria was created by the first Elder Dragon, who then brought the other Elder Dragons into being.[^gw2timeline] These titanic entities feed on magic and rise in cycles, devastating the world before returning to dormancy. To survive their reign, five elder races, the **Seers**, the **Forgotten**, the dwarves, the jotun, and the mursaat, allied in resistance. The Seers found a way to hide magic from the dragons, crafting first the Shadowstone and then the **Bloodstone**; around 1680 BE the Forgotten freed the dragon-champion Glint from the Elder Dragon Kralkatorrik's control, and with the world's magic concealed the dragons sank into a long slumber.[^gw2timeline]

### The coming of humanity and the Six Gods

Humanity is not native to Tyria. Around 786 BE the **Six Human Gods** first appeared in Cantha, bringing humans who spread north and west across the world over the following centuries, settling Elona, Orr, and finally Ascalon.[^gw2timeline] The gods granted their followers favor and, in time, magic; that gift would prove difficult to contain.

### The gift of magic and the Exodus

In 1 BE the god **Abaddon** gave magic freely to every race of Tyria, and the wars that followed grew so destructive that the other gods sealed magic away inside the Bloodstone.[^gw2timeline] Abaddon rebelled against this decision and assaulted his fellow gods; he was defeated and imprisoned in the Realm of Torment, an act that shattered the Crystal Sea into the Crystal Desert. In the aftermath the gods withdrew from the mortal world in the event known as the **Exodus of the Gods**, which marks year 0 of the Mouvelian calendar and the boundary between the BE and AE eras.[^gw2timeline]

## The Searing and the end of the Guild Wars (1070 AE)

The original game's story proper begins in 1070 AE, in an Ascalon already worn down by a long human conflict.[^gw1timeline]

### The Guild Wars and pre-Searing Ascalon

The wars that give the series its name were a long struggle among the human kingdoms of Tyria, chiefly Ascalon, Kryta, and Orr, fought as the influence of the gods receded. *Prophecies* opens in a peaceful, pre-Searing Ascalon that introduces the player to the world before catastrophe arrives.[^gw1timeline]

### The charr invasion and the Searing

In 1070 AE the **charr**, long held north of Ascalon's Great Northern Wall, unleashed the **Searing**: a bombardment of flaming crystals called down through their worship of the Titans, which reduced Ascalon's green country to a scorched waste and broke the human kingdoms' power.[^gw1timeline] The disaster ended the Guild Wars by force, since the surviving kingdoms could no longer afford to fight one another. In the chaos, the **White Mantle** seized control of Kryta.[^gw1timeline]

## Prophecies: the White Mantle and the Door of Komalie (1072 AE)

Two years after the Searing, the surviving Ascalonians and their allies follow a prophecy across the continent.[^gw1timeline]

### Flight to Kryta and the Shining Blade

Ascalonian refugees fled south to Kryta, where the ruling White Mantle outwardly offered sanctuary while secretly serving the **mursaat**, the same ancient race that had once warred with the Seers.[^gw1timeline] Allied with the rebel Shining Blade, the player uncovers the Mantle's practice of sacrificing the "Chosen" to sustain the mursaat's hidden power.

### The Ring of Fire and the Door of Komalie

The trail leads to the volcanic Ring of Fire Islands, where the undead lich Khilbron engineers the opening of the **Door of Komalie**, releasing the Titans he intends to command.[^gw1timeline] The campaign ends with the assault to stop him at Abaddon's Mouth, a confrontation that foreshadows the fallen god whose name the place bears.

## Factions: plague and war in Cantha (1072 AE)

In the same year, far to the east, the island empire of **Cantha** faced a catastrophe of its own.[^gw1timeline]

### The return of Shiro Tagachi

Two centuries earlier the assassin **Shiro Tagachi** had murdered the Canthan emperor and, in dying, unleashed the Jade Wind that petrified the Jade Sea and the Echovald Forest. In 1072 AE Shiro returned as a spectral Envoy, and his presence spread a corrupting plague, the **Affliction**, across the empire.[^gw1timeline]

### The alliance and Shiro's defeat

With the monk Mhenlo and the scholar Master Togo, the player rallies the long-feuding Luxon and Kurzick factions and pursues Shiro to the Imperial Sanctum, where he is finally destroyed and the plague's source removed.[^gw1timeline]

## Nightfall: Abaddon's return in Elona (1075 AE)

Three years later the southern realm of **Elona** became the stage for a threat reaching back to the gods themselves.[^gw1timeline]

### Varesh Ossa and the heralds of Nightfall

The Kournan warmarshal Varesh Ossa, secretly a servant of the imprisoned Abaddon, sought to bring about **Nightfall**: the merging of the Realm of Torment with the mortal world.[^gw1timeline] Newly raised as a Sunspear, the player works to stop her before the ritual can complete.

### The Realm of Torment and Kormir's ascension

Pursuing Varesh into the Realm of Torment, the player's company confronts Abaddon directly. With the Sunspear leader **Kormir**, Abaddon is defeated, and Kormir takes on his divine power to ascend as the new goddess of truth.[^gw1timeline] In the same period the undead ruler **Palawa Joko**, long imprisoned beneath the desert, is set free, a loose thread that *Guild Wars 2* would eventually resolve.[^gw1timeline]

## Eye of the North: the Destroyers and a new age (1078 AE)

The series' only true expansion turns away from the human gods and toward an older menace, and it deliberately sets the stage for the sequel.[^gw1timeline]

### The Great Destroyer and Primordus

Earthquakes and eruptions of **Destroyers**, creatures of living magma, herald the stirring of the Elder Dragon **Primordus** deep beneath Tyria.[^gw1timeline] The player descends into the depths to defeat the Great Destroyer, though Primordus itself only stirs rather than fully waking, leaving the larger threat unresolved.[^gw1timeline]

### The Asura, Norn, and the charr rebellion

The upheaval drives three of *Guild Wars 2*'s future playable races into contact with the surface world: the **asura** emerge from their underground cities, the **norn** range south from the Shiverpeaks, and within the charr, **Pyre Fierceshot** leads a warband in rebellion against the Titan-worshipping Shaman caste.[^gw1timeline] These threads establish the alliances and grievances that shape the sequel's world.

### The Hall of Monuments

The expansion introduces the **Hall of Monuments**, a personal shrine in which players record their achievements, companions, and treasures. Roughly two and a half centuries later, those records translate into cosmetic rewards for the same account in *Guild Wars 2*, making the Hall the literal bridge between the two games.[^gw2main]

## Guild Wars Beyond: the bridge to a sequel (1079–1080 AE)

After the boxed campaigns, ArenaNet continued the story through free updates collectively called **Guild Wars Beyond**, which moved the world toward the sequel's status quo.[^gw1timeline]

### The War in Kryta

The **War in Kryta** saw the White Mantle attempt a violent resurgence, broken in the end by the Shining Blade and the restoration of Queen Salma to the Krytan throne; that lineage still rules Kryta in *Guild Wars 2*.[^gw1timeline]

### Winds of Change

In Cantha, the **Winds of Change** arc followed the Ministry of Purity's increasingly authoritarian campaign to wipe out the lingering Affliction, foreshadowing the isolationist empire that players would not revisit until *End of Dragons*.[^gw1timeline] By 1080 AE the Ebon Vanguard had withdrawn north to fortify Ebonhawke, and Tyria stood on the threshold of the Elder Dragons' return.[^gw1timeline]
