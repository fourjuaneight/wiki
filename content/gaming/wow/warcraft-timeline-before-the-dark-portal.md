The **Warcraft** timeline measures recorded history against a single fixed point: the opening of the **Dark Portal**, the gateway that first carried the orcish Horde from Draenor to the world of **Azeroth**. Events before that moment are dated *Before the Dark Portal* (BDP) and events after it *After the Dark Portal* (ADP), the convention Blizzard's official sources use throughout the *Warcraft Chronicle* volumes and tie-in fiction.[^timeline2026] This entry covers the BDP span — from the birth of the cosmos to the eve of the First War — reconstructed from in-game lore, the strategy games, and the novels.[^wowpedia2026]

Because dates this far back are estimates, most are given as spans rather than exact years, and the deepest mythological events carry no date at all. The companion entry, *Warcraft Timeline: After the Dark Portal*, picks up at Year 0.

## The birth of the cosmos

The universe formed from the eternal clash of the **Light** and the **Void**. From that tension arose two opposed forces that shape every later conflict: the ordering, life-giving principle embodied by the **titans** — colossal world-souls slumbering within nascent planets — and the corrupting hunger embodied by the **Old Gods**, seeded across the cosmos by the Void Lords to twist those world-souls before they could wake. The first titans formed the **Pantheon**, while in the chaotic realm of the **Twisting Nether** the first demons arose.[^timeline2026]

### Sargeras and the Burning Legion

The Pantheon's greatest warrior, **Sargeras**, was charged with safeguarding creation from demonic incursion. After discovering a nascent world-soul already corrupted by the Void, he concluded that all such souls were doomed and that creation itself was irredeemable. He turned against his former kin, slew that world-soul, and forged the **Burning Legion** — an endless demonic army dedicated to unmaking reality before the Void could claim it. The Legion's crusade is the engine behind nearly every catastrophe that befalls Azeroth.[^timeline2026]

## The coming of the Old Gods

Long before the titans found Azeroth, the Old Gods arrived and embedded themselves in its crust, enslaving the **Elemental Lords** and raising the Black Empire across the surface. Their presence was not merely an occupation; it was a slow corruption of the planet's sleeping world-soul, the very thing the Void Lords had sent them to capture.[^timeline2026]

## The ordering of Azeroth

When the Pantheon at last discovered Azeroth, the titans recognized the world-soul within and moved to protect it. They could not safely kill the Old Gods without killing the planet, so they created the **titan-forged** — durable servants of living stone and metal, including the Keepers — to wage war on the Black Empire and chain the Old Gods deep beneath the surface. With the threat contained, the Keepers shaped the continents, seeded life, and established the Dragon Aspects as guardians of the world.[^timeline2026]

### The Curse of Flesh

The Old Gods' final sabotage was the **Curse of Flesh**, a corrupting affliction that gradually turned the metal-and-stone titan-forged into beings of soft, mutable flesh. Over millennia it transformed entire lineages: the vrykul whose stunted offspring became humans, the mechagnomes who became gnomes, the earthen who became dwarves, and the mogu of Pandaria. Far from a simple disease, the curse is the origin of most of Azeroth's mortal races.[^timeline2026]

## The Dragon Aspects and the War of the Scaleborn

Around 20,000 years BDP, the Keepers ended the reign of the monstrous proto-dragon **Galakrond** and empowered five proto-dragons as the **Dragon Aspects**, each granted dominion over an aspect of the world by a member of the Pantheon. Their rule was soon challenged in the **War of the Scaleborn**, a long conflict against the Primal Incarnates — dragons who rejected titanic ordering in favor of raw elemental power. The Aspects ultimately sealed the Incarnates away on the Dragon Isles, which were then hidden from the world.[^timeline2026]

## The troll empires and the Aqir Wars

As mortal civilization spread, the trolls rose to dominate primordial Kalimdor, splintering into the Zandalari, Amani, and Gurubashi. Their great rival was the insectoid **aqir**, servants of the Old Gods. The trolls' Empire of Zul eventually shattered the aqir empire, scattering the survivors into three colonies — Ahn'Qiraj, Azjol-Nerub, and the mantid hold of Manti'vess — each of which would resurface as a threat in later ages.[^timeline2026]

## The mogu and pandaren empires

In the lands later called Pandaria, the Curse of Flesh fractured the mogu into warring clans until the warlord **Lei Shen**, the Thunder King, seized power and forged a brutal empire that enslaved the region's other races. His dynasty endured for thousands of years before the slave **Kang** led the Pandaren Revolution, overthrowing the mogu and founding the Pandaren Empire.[^timeline2026]

## The night elves and the Well of Eternity

A tribe of dark trolls that settled beside a vast font of arcane power in the heart of Kalimdor was transformed over generations into the **kaldorei**, or night elves. The wellspring at the center of their world — the **Well of Eternity** — granted them extraordinary power and longevity but bred a dangerous dependence. A faction of nobility, the Highborne, embraced arcane magic without restraint under their queen, **Azshara**, ignoring the warnings of those who feared its corrupting pull.[^timeline2026]

## The War of the Ancients and the Sundering

Roughly 10,000 years BDP, the Highborne's reckless magic drew the attention of Sargeras, who corrupted Azshara and opened a portal through which the Burning Legion poured into Azeroth, laying waste to night-elven civilization in the **War of the Ancients**.[^timeline2026]

### The Sundering

A resistance led by **Malfurion Stormrage**, **Tyrande Whisperwind**, and Malfurion's brother **Illidan Stormrage** destroyed the Well of Eternity to deny Sargeras entry. The Well's collapse triggered the **Sundering**, a cataclysm that drove some eighty percent of Kalimdor's landmass beneath the sea and split the single supercontinent into the separate landmasses of the present day. Far to the east, Emperor Shaohao chose this moment to seal Pandaria behind an eternal veil of mist, hiding it from the world for ten thousand years.[^timeline2026]

### Mount Hyjal and Nordrassil

In the aftermath, the night elves planted the first World Tree, **Nordrassil**, atop Mount Hyjal to anchor their renewed immortality. Illidan, unwilling to surrender the Well's power, secretly created a new font from stolen waters; for this betrayal he was imprisoned for ten thousand years.[^timeline2026]

## The long vigil and the sundered world

The night elves withdrew into a long age of isolation. Their druids entered the **Emerald Dream** to slumber and watch over the world's natural cycles, while Tyrande's Sentinels guarded the waking realm. The exiled Highborne, banished for their continued use of arcane magic, sailed east to the Eastern Kingdoms, where their skin lost its violet hue and they founded the kingdom of **Quel'Thalas**, becoming the high elves.[^timeline2026]

## The rise of humanity: Arathor and the Troll Wars

The descendants of cursed vrykul — humans — eventually united the northern lands into the kingdom of **Arathor**. When the Amani trolls threatened both peoples, the humans of Arathor allied with the high elves of Quel'Thalas, and in return for their aid the elves taught arcane magic to a hundred human students. That gift later drew demons to Azeroth, prompting the mages of Dalaran to form the secret **Council of Tirisfal** and to empower a single champion, the **Guardian**, to fight the Legion in the shadows. The mantle passed in time from Alodi through Aegwynn to her son, Medivh.[^timeline2026]

## The awakening of the dwarves

The dwarves woke from titan-forged slumber within Uldaman, migrated west, and founded **Khaz Modan** with its great capital of Ironforge, aiding the nearby gnomes in raising Gnomeregan. Their unity later fractured in the **War of the Three Hammers**, fought among the Bronzebeard, Wildhammer, and Dark Iron clans for control of Ironforge; the Bronzebeards prevailed, the Wildhammers relocated, and the defeated Dark Irons fell under the dominion of the firelord **Ragnaros** beneath Blackrock Mountain.[^timeline2026]

## Aegwynn and the hunt for Sargeras

The Guardian **Aegwynn** confronted and destroyed an avatar of Sargeras in the frozen north, burying its remains and believing the threat ended. In truth, Sargeras' spirit hid itself within her, waiting. When she conceived a son, **Medivh**, and hid the Guardian's power inside him, she unwittingly delivered Sargeras a vessel — a corruption that would erupt when the boy came of age.[^timeline2026]

## The rise of the orcish clans

On the distant world of Draenor, the orcs emerged from subterranean caverns and spread across the land as a shamanistic clan society in harmony with the elements. They shared the world with the **draenei**, a refugee remnant of the eredar who had fled Sargeras' corruption aboard the dimensional ship Genedar under their prophet, **Velen**. For a time the two peoples coexisted in wary peace.[^timeline2026]

## Gul'dan and the corruption of the Horde

The demon lord Kil'jaeden, hunting Velen across the cosmos, manipulated the orc elder shaman Ner'zhul and then his apprentice **Gul'dan**, granting Gul'dan fel magic and making him the first orcish warlock. Gul'dan founded the secret **Shadow Council** and engineered the unification of the scattered clans into a single, militarized **Horde** under the warchief Blackhand.[^timeline2026]

### The genocide of the draenei

Goaded by demonic manipulation and blamed for atrocities they did not commit, the orc clans turned on the draenei in a campaign of extermination, driving them from their cities and corrupting holy sites such as the Temple of Karabor, which became the Black Temple. Survivors who were warped by fel energies degenerated into the Broken and the Lost Ones.[^timeline2026]

### The Blood of Mannoroth

To bind the Horde irrevocably to the Legion, Gul'dan led the clans to drink the blood of the pit lord **Mannoroth**, which empowered them, turned their skin green, and enslaved their will to the demon. The Frostwolf clan and a quarantined population in Nagrad refused; the latter, untouched by the fel corruption, became known as the **Mag'har**, the "uncorrupted."[^timeline2026]

## The building of the Dark Portal

His powers fully awakened and his mind dominated by Sargeras, Medivh reached across the cosmos to Gul'dan, revealed the rich and undefended world of Azeroth, and offered the Horde a path to it. Together they raised the **Dark Portal** — a matched gateway anchored on both worlds in the swamp of the Black Morass on Azeroth and Tanaan Jungle on Draenor. Its opening marks Year 0 and the beginning of the events covered in the companion entry.[^timeline2026]

## Release Reference

No playable game is set before the Dark Portal; the *Warcraft* series begins with the First War at Year 0. The history in this entry was instead established through Blizzard's lore compendia and tie-in novels, listed below with the eras they cover and their real-world publication dates.
